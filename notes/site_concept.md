# Site Concept

## Purpose

This site presents Naoyuki Hirakawa's public-safe technical portfolio for LLM-assisted infrastructure design lifecycle work.

The goal is to explain practical AI workflow engineering for readers who care about deployment, success, infrastructure architecture, reviewability, and human governance.

## Audience

- AI deployment teams.
- AI success teams.
- Infrastructure architects.
- Network engineers.
- Technical reviewers.
- Enterprise workflow designers.
- Hiring reviewers.

## Content Boundaries

- English only for public-facing pages.
- No private project data.
- No real customer names.
- No real person names except Naoyuki Hirakawa.
- No real meeting transcripts.
- No real network diagrams.
- No real review comments.
- No real IP addresses.
- No remote or publication action without human approval.

## Future Article Ideas

- Designing source manifests for LLM workflows.
- Semantic delta detection in engineering review.
- Approval checklists as workflow artifacts.
- From Markdown workflow to CLI.
- From CLI to API-backed workflow execution.
- What AI success teams need from technical artifacts.

## Update Policy

Each update should preserve the public-safe boundary, avoid exaggerated claims, and connect technical content back to reviewable workflow practice.
